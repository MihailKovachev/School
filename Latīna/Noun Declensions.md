	||Singulāris|Plūrālis|

	|Nōminātīvus|**-**|**-ēs**/ **-a** (n.)|
	|Genetīvus|**-is**|**-um**|
	|Datīvus|**-ī**|**-ibus**|
	|Accūsātīvus|**-em** / **-** (n.)|**-ēs** / **-a** (n.)|
	|Ablātīvus|**-e**|**-ībus**|
	|Vocātīvus|-|-|
	|Locātīvus|**-ī**|**-ibus**|
	
	**a) i-stems**
	- masculine and feminine nouns - the nominative ends in "-is" and the word is parisyllabic (equal number of syllables in the nominative and genitive singular); the words *imber*, *ūter*, *linter*, *venter*
	- neuter nouns - the nominative ends in "-e", "-al", or "-ar"
	
	||Singulāris|Plūrālis|
	|:--:|:---:|:----:|
	|Nōminātīvus|**-**|**-ēs**/ **-ia** (n.)|
	|Genetīvus|**-is**|**-ium**|
	|Datīvus|**-ī**|**-ibus**|
	|Accūsātīvus|**-em** / **-im**/ **-** (n.)|**-ēs** / **-īs** / **-ia** (n.)|
	|Ablātīvus|**-ī** / **-e**|**-ībus**|
	
	- [ ] mixed i-stems - only have the plural i-stems endings:
		- nouns ending in "-ēs"
		- monosyllabics ending in a consonant + "-s"/"-x"
		- polysyllabics ending in "-ns" / "-rs"
		- nouns ending in "-tās"
		- nouns ending in "-ās"/"-īs" which denote places born or lived
		- the words dōs, fraud, glīs, līs, mās, mūs, nix, nox, strix, vīs
