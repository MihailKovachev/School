1. Vowels - long and short
	
	**a) short vowels**
	- before another vowel or **h**: via, trahō
	- before **-nt-** and **-nd-**: laudant, dūcentis
	- before **-m**, **-t**, **-r**, and **-l** at the end of a word: amor, docet, audiēbam, animal
	
	**b) long vowels**
	- before **-nf-**, **-ns-**, and **-gn-**: cōnferō, mēnsa, māgnus
	- the genitive form **-īus** after a consonant: utrīus, nūllīus
	- vowels formed from contrations: nihil -> nīl; māvolō -> mālō
